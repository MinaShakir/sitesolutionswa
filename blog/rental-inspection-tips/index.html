How to Pass Your Rental Inspection: Tips from a Professional Perth Inspector

At Site & Strata Solutions WA, we see hundreds of properties across Perth. Whether it’s a routine check or a final vacate inspection, the goal is always the same: a well-maintained home and a protected bond. We’ve compiled the top areas where tenants often lose points—and how you can stay ahead of them.

1. First Impressions: The Exterior

In Western Australia, our sandy soil and dry heat make garden maintenance a challenge. Inspectors often make their assessment before even entering the home.

  • Weeding Ensure limestone garden beds and paving are completely free of weeds.
  • Cobwebs Use a long-handled broom to clear eaves, carports, and window frames.
  • Bins Wash out your wheelie bins; it’s a small detail that shows the inspector the property is cared for.

2. The "Hidden" Dust Traps

Inspectors look where most people forget to clean. These are the top reasons for follow-up inspections:

  • Exhaust Fans Dust builds up quickly in bathroom and kitchen fans. A quick wipe prevents motor strain and looks great.
  • Sliding Door Tracks Vacuum out the sand and debris from your tracks so they glide smoothly.

3. Wet Areas & Mold Prevention

Perth winters can be damp, leading to moisture issues in high-use areas.

  • Grout Use a soft brush to ensure bathroom grout is free of soap scum or orange mold.
  • Under Sinks Check cupboards under kitchen and bathroom sinks for any slow leaks or moisture damage.

4. Walls and Floor Protection

  • Scuffs Use a "magic eraser" for light wall scuffs, but be careful not to rub off the paint.
  • Furniture Pads Ensure heavy furniture has felt pads to avoid scratching floorboards or marking tiles.
